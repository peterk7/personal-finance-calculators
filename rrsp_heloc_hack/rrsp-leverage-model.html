Borrow-to-contribute · two-world analysis

RRSP Leverage vs. Investing the Same Cash

World 1 — borrow a lump sum in December, drop it into your RRSP, start repaying immediately, and use the tax refund (~4 months later) as a lump-sum paydown.
World 2 — skip the loan; invest the identical out-of-pocket dollars in a taxable account instead.

Model. World 1: full contribution compounds tax-free from December; monthly payment (sized to amortize the post-refund residual over the payoff period) starts January; months 1–4 add extra interest on the refund portion of the balance; refund arrives at month 4 and clears that portion; base payment continues through month N (= payoff period); loan finishes exactly on schedule. World 2: the identical out-of-pocket cash-flows (same monthly payment, same number of months) are invested in a taxable account at the same return rate; on liquidation, only capital gains are taxed at 50% inclusion × your withdrawal marginal rate. Both shown after tax, fully liquidated, in nominal dollars. Not tax advice — pressure-test with your accountant.
